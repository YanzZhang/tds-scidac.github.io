The TDS Mission

The primary objective of the Tokamak Disruption Simulation (TDS) SciDAC Center is to help establish the physics basis for disruption mitigation in a reactor-size tokamak through large-scale simulations, using the largest computers available.

Tokamak Disruption

Tokamak disruption, if not completely avoided, must be effectively mitigated in a fusion reactor like ITER to prevent:

Thermal loads to the wall

Rapid erosion of wall surfaces through melting, vaporization, and sublimation can occur due to the orders of magnitude increase in the power exhaust in the thermal quench phase.

Damaging electromagnetic forces

Breaking and shifting of the vacuum vessel and blanket modules can occur due to the extreme electromagnetic loading by eddy and halo currents in the current quench phase.

Runaway electrons

Deep damage of surface and substrate of the plasma facing components caused by runaway electrons that can induce costly secondary damage.

TDS Research

Tokamak disruption, if not completely avoided, must be effectively mitigated in a fusion reactor like ITER to prevent:

Thermal loads to the wall

Transport (gyrokinetic and gyrofluid) calculations of particle and energy in 3D magnetic fields.

Damaging electromagnetic forces

Multi-fluid and fluid/kinetic calculation of plasma/neutral dynamics.

Runaway electrons

Kinetic calculations of runaway generation and transport.
